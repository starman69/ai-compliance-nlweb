# ISO/IEC 23894:2023 — AI — Guidance on Risk Management

> **Open, non-normative summary.** Not the standard text (copyrighted by ISO/IEC; purchase
> from ISO/ANSI/BSI). Compiled from public information about the standard's scope and
> structure so the corpus can route and answer high-level risk-management questions. For
> citable detail, consult the licensed standard. Issued February 2023 by **ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42**.

## Scope and purpose
ISO/IEC 23894 provides **guidance** (it is informative, not a requirements/certification
standard) on how organizations that develop, deploy, or use AI can **manage risk
specifically related to AI**. It adapts the general risk-management approach of **ISO
31000:2018** to the AI context, and is designed to integrate with an organization's
existing risk management and with the AIMS of **ISO/IEC 42001**.

## Structure
The document mirrors ISO 31000's three pillars and adds AI-specific elaboration:

- **Principles** — the foundations of effective risk management, framed for AI (value
  creation and protection, integration, structured/comprehensive process, etc.).
- **Framework** — leadership and commitment, integration, design, implementation,
  evaluation, and improvement of the risk-management framework within the organization.
- **Process** — communication and consultation; scope, context, and criteria; **risk
  assessment** (identification, analysis, evaluation); **risk treatment**; monitoring and
  review; recording and reporting — applied to AI systems across their life cycle.
- **Annexes** — informative material on AI-related **objectives** and **risk sources**
  (e.g., issues arising from data, model behavior, transparency, robustness, and human
  oversight) and on mapping risk management across the AI life cycle.

## Cross-references
- **ISO 31000:2018** — the parent enterprise risk-management standard it specializes.
- **ISO/IEC 42001** — Clause 6 risk activities and Annex A draw on this guidance.
- **ISO/IEC 22989** — shared terminology.
- **NIST AI RMF** — the closest functional analogue; "compare ISO 23894 vs NIST AI RMF" is
  a common request (both are voluntary, risk-based, lifecycle-oriented).
