# Singapore — Model AI Governance Framework (2nd Edition, 2020)

A non-binding, accountability-based guidance published by Singapore's PDPC and IMDA (2nd
edition, 21 January 2020) to help private-sector organizations deploy AI responsibly. It is
sector- and technology-agnostic.

## Guiding principles
1. AI-assisted decision-making should be **explainable, transparent, and fair**.
2. AI systems should be **human-centric** and safe.

## Four key areas
1. **Internal governance structures and measures** — clear roles and accountability to manage
   AI risks; train staff; establish risk-management and oversight processes.
2. **Determining the level of human involvement** in AI-augmented decision-making — a
   risk-based approach (human-in-the-loop / over-the-loop / out-of-the-loop) calibrated to the
   probability and severity of harm.
3. **Operations management** — data quality and lineage, model robustness, reproducibility and
   auditability, and minimizing bias across the AI development lifecycle.
4. **Stakeholder interaction and communication** — transparency with users and broader networks
   of intermediaries/partners, disclosure, and channels for feedback and redress.

## Companion tools
The framework is supported by the **Implementation and Self-Assessment Guide for Organisations
(ISAGO)** and a **Compendium of Use Cases** illustrating real-world adoption. (Singapore later
released AI Verify and a Model AI Governance Framework for Generative AI.)
