# Microsoft Responsible AI

## Core commitment
Microsoft commits to responsibly designing, building, and releasing AI technologies with
humans at the center, distilled into six foundational principles that guide AI development
and deployment.

## Six core principles
1. **Fairness** — AI systems should treat all individuals equitably and avoid discrimination
   based on personal characteristics.
2. **Reliability and Safety** — systems should perform consistently and safely across their
   intended use cases without causing harm.
3. **Privacy and Security** — protect personal data and maintain confidentiality of sensitive
   information across the AI lifecycle.
4. **Inclusiveness** — design AI that serves diverse populations and reflects varied
   perspectives and needs.
5. **Transparency** — make clear how systems function, what data they use, and how decisions
   are made.
6. **Accountability** — establish clear ownership and responsibility for AI system outcomes
   and impacts.

## Governance and standards
- **Responsible AI Standard** — Microsoft's internal framework operationalizing the principles
  (fairness, reliability, privacy, inclusiveness) across AI initiatives.
- **Office of Responsible AI** — oversees AI ethics and governance company-wide.
- **Responsible AI Dashboard** — tooling for end-to-end assessment, monitoring, and management
  of AI systems.

## Implementation practices
Establish cross-department stakeholder engagement; train staff on responsible-AI principles;
validate models for fairness and real-world alignment; maintain human oversight in critical
decisions; and ensure compliance with applicable legal/regulatory requirements (including the
EU AI Act).

## Tools and resources
Human-AI eXperience (HAX) Workbook for human-AI interaction best practices; Azure AI tooling
for security, safety, and privacy; and regular Responsible AI transparency reporting.

## Policy and collaboration
Microsoft engages the Frontier Model Forum, the Partnership on AI (synthetic-media framework),
and UNESCO, and supports alignment with emerging regulation such as the EU AI Act.
