# UK ICO — Guidance on AI and Data Protection

Guidance from the UK Information Commissioner's Office (updated March 2023) on applying UK GDPR
to AI systems. It is **structured around the data-protection principles** and is central to the
ICO's role under the UK's pro-innovation, regulator-led approach to AI.

## Accountability and governance
Organizations must address the accountability and governance implications of AI, including
**Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)**. A DPIA should evidence that **less-risky
alternatives** were considered and explain why they were not chosen, and clarify controller /
processor roles in the AI supply chain.

## Lawfulness
A chapter on lawfulness addresses **inferences, affinity groups, and special-category data** —
identifying the appropriate lawful basis when AI generates or uses such data.

## Fairness
Fairness in AI covers how data-protection fairness applies to AI and the key questions to ask
about **automated decision-making under Article 22 UK GDPR**, including statistical accuracy and
bias/discrimination.

## Transparency
A standalone transparency chapter: where personal data is collected directly from individuals,
**privacy information must be provided before** the data is used to train a model or apply it to
those individuals.

## Individual rights and automated decisions
The UK GDPR's provisions on **profiling and automated decision-making (ADM)** are highly
relevant to AI — including the right to obtain human intervention, to express a view, and to
contest solely-automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects.
