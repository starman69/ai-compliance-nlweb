# FedRAMP — Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program

A US government-wide program providing a **standardized approach to security assessment,
authorization, and continuous monitoring** for cloud products and services used by federal
agencies. Established in 2011 (OMB); the cloud analogue of FISMA. Principle: **"do once, use
many times"** — one authorization is reusable across agencies.

## Security baselines
FedRAMP control requirements are derived from **NIST SP 800-53** (as revised), plus cloud-
specific control enhancements, organized into **Low / Moderate / High** impact baselines
(with a Tailored Low-Impact SaaS "LI-SaaS"). A cloud service handling federal data must hold a
FedRAMP authorization.

## Authorization paths
- **JAB Provisional ATO (P-ATO)** — granted by the Joint Authorization Board (CIOs of DHS, GSA,
  DoD).
- **Agency ATO** — an individual federal agency authorizes a cloud service for its use.

## Roles
- **FedRAMP PMO** (within GSA) — runs the program day-to-day and maintains the Marketplace of
  authorized Cloud Service Offerings.
- **Third-Party Assessment Organizations (3PAOs)** — accredited independent assessors that test
  and verify a provider's control implementation.
- **OMB** (policy), **NIST** (standards/800-53), **DHS** (continuous-monitoring strategy,
  incident response).

## Continuous monitoring
Authorization is ongoing: providers must continuously monitor security, report on control
status, and feed threat/incident data — sustaining compliance across the service lifecycle.
