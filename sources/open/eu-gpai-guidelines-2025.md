# EU — Commission Guidelines on the scope of obligations for GPAI models (2025)

Guidelines published by the European Commission on **18 July 2025** clarifying how the EU AI
Act applies to **general-purpose AI (GPAI) models**: the definition and scope, lifecycle
obligations, systemic-risk criteria, and notification duties for providers.

## Definition and scope
A GPAI model is one trained with significant compute — indicatively **>10²³ FLOPs** — and able
to generate language (text/audio), text-to-image, or text-to-video, and competently perform a
wide range of distinct tasks.

## Obligations for GPAI providers
- Maintain detailed **technical documentation** for the model.
- Publish a **summary of training content** (using the Commission's template) and a copyright
  policy; comply with **EU copyright law**.
- Share information with the **AI Office** and with downstream providers integrating the model.

## Systemic-risk GPAI
A model trained with **≥10²⁵ FLOPs** is presumed to have high-impact capabilities and may be
designated **systemic-risk** GPAI. Additional duties: standardized **model evaluation** and
**adversarial testing**, Union-level systemic-risk assessment and mitigation, cybersecurity
protection, and **serious-incident** monitoring and reporting to the AI Office and national
authorities.

## Timeline
GPAI obligations applied from **2 August 2025**; the Commission's enforcement powers apply from
**2 August 2026**. Adherence to the GPAI Code of Practice is a means of demonstrating
compliance.
