# UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (2021)

The first **global standard-setting instrument** on AI ethics, adopted by all UNESCO member
states in **November 2021**. It takes a human-rights-centred approach and is implemented
through concrete policy tools.

## Four core values
1. Respect, protection, and promotion of **human rights and human dignity** and fundamental
   freedoms.
2. **Living in peaceful, just, and interconnected societies.**
3. Ensuring **diversity and inclusiveness**.
4. **Environment and ecosystem flourishing.**

## Ten core principles
1. **Proportionality and do no harm** — AI use must not exceed what is necessary for a
   legitimate aim; assess risks to prevent harm.
2. **Safety and security** — avoid and address safety risks and security vulnerabilities.
3. **Fairness and non-discrimination** — advance social justice; benefits must reach all.
4. **Sustainability** — assess impacts against goals such as the UN SDGs.
5. **Right to privacy and data protection** — across the AI lifecycle, with adequate frameworks.
6. **Human oversight and determination** — humans retain ultimate responsibility and accountability.
7. **Transparency and explainability** — appropriate to the context and use.
8. **Responsibility and accountability** — AI should be auditable and traceable, with oversight
   and impact-assessment mechanisms.
9. **Awareness and literacy** — promote public understanding through education and engagement.
10. **Multi-stakeholder and adaptive governance and collaboration** — respect international law
    and sovereignty; enable diverse participation.

## Policy areas and tools
Eleven policy action areas (data governance, environment, gender, education/research, health
and social well-being, etc.). Implementation tools include the **Ethical Impact Assessment
(EIA)** and the **Readiness Assessment Methodology (RAM)** that helps member states gauge their
preparedness to implement the Recommendation.
