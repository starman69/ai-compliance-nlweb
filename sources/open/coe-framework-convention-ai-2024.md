# Council of Europe Framework Convention on Artificial Intelligence

Framework Convention on Artificial Intelligence and Human Rights, Democracy and the Rule of
Law (CETS No. 225), opened for signature 5 September 2024 — the **first legally binding
international treaty** on artificial intelligence.

## Purpose and scope
Ensures that activities within the lifecycle of AI systems are fully consistent with **human
rights, democracy, and the rule of law**, addressing risks such as disinformation,
algorithmic discrimination, and threats to public institutions. It applies to public
authorities and to private actors acting on their behalf; national security and defence
activities are excluded.

## Core principles
Parties must ensure AI activities respect:
- **Human dignity and individual autonomy**
- **Transparency and oversight** of AI systems
- **Accountability and responsibility** for adverse impacts
- **Equality and non-discrimination**, including gender equality
- **Privacy and personal data protection**
- **Reliability** of AI systems
- **Safe innovation** (e.g. controlled environments / regulatory sandboxes)

## Structure
Eight chapters, 26 articles: general provisions; obligations to protect human rights and the
integrity of democratic processes; the core principles (Arts. 6–13); **remedies and procedural
safeguards** for affected persons (Arts. 14–15); a **risk and impact management** framework;
implementation; a Conference of the Parties for cooperation and follow-up; and final clauses.

## Risk and impact management
Parties must adopt measures to identify, assess, prevent, and mitigate risks to human rights,
democracy, and the rule of law across the AI lifecycle — including, where appropriate,
moratoria or bans on certain applications. Enters into force three months after the fifth
ratification (including at least three Council of Europe member states).

## Signatories
Signed/endorsed by 50+ states and entities, including the European Union, United Kingdom,
United States, Canada, Japan, Israel, Norway, Switzerland, Ukraine, and others.
