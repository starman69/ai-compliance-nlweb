# Brazil — AI Bill (PL 2338/2023)

Bill No. 2338/2023 establishes rules for the development and use of artificial intelligence in
Brazil under a **risk-based framework** balancing innovation with the protection of fundamental
rights. Approved by the Federal Senate on **10 December 2024**; advanced to the Chamber of
Deputies.

## Risk-based classification
- **Excessive-risk / prohibited** — AI must not use subliminal techniques that induce harmful
  behaviour, nor exploit vulnerabilities of specific groups (e.g. by age or disability); social
  scoring and certain biometric uses are restricted.
- **High-risk** — systems used in critical infrastructure, law enforcement, administration of
  justice, credit scoring, employment, education, and medical diagnosis. These require
  **algorithmic impact assessments**, robustness/accuracy/reliability testing, and **human
  oversight**, plus governance and documentation obligations.

## Rights of affected persons
- Right to **know** when an AI system is making or supporting a decision that affects them.
- Right to **explanation** and to **human review** of significant automated decisions.
- Protection against **discriminatory bias** and preservation of **privacy**.
- In some cases, review by external/independent experts.

## Governance
Providers and deployers carry graduated obligations by risk level; a competent authority
(coordinated with the ANPD, Brazil's data-protection authority) oversees supervision,
incident reporting, and enforcement.
