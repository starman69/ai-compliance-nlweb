# ENISA — Multilayer Framework for Good Cybersecurity Practices for AI (FAICP)

Published by the EU Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA) on 7 June 2023. Guidance for national
competent authorities and AI stakeholders to **secure AI systems, operations, and processes**
using existing knowledge and best practices — a scalable, step-by-step approach to building
trustworthy AI and identifying gaps in current cybersecurity practice.

## Three-layer architecture
1. **Cybersecurity foundations (ICT base)** — baseline protections and standard cybersecurity
   controls applicable to any organization (the general ICT environment the AI runs on).
2. **AI-specific cybersecurity** — security challenges unique to AI: model robustness, data
   integrity, and resistance to adversarial attacks (poisoning, evasion, model extraction).
3. **Sector-specific cybersecurity for AI** — contextualized practices for the particular
   industry/regulatory environment in which the AI system operates.

## Key characteristics
- **Scalable** to organizations of varying maturity and resources.
- **Knowledge consolidation** — leverages established frameworks and best practices.
- **Gap identification** — pinpoints missing elements in current AI security approaches.
- **Holistic** — spans ICT foundations, AI-specific concerns, and sector context.

Target audience: national authorities, EU bodies, and private-sector organizations deploying
AI systems.
