# ISO/IEC 22989:2022 — AI — Concepts and Terminology

> **Open, non-normative summary.** Not the standard text (copyrighted by ISO/IEC; purchase
> from ISO/ANSI/BSI). Compiled from public information so the corpus can ground
> definitional questions. For exact, citable definitions, consult the licensed standard.
> Issued July 2022 by **ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42**.

## Scope and purpose
ISO/IEC 22989 establishes the **foundational terminology and concepts** for artificial
intelligence, providing a common vocabulary used across the SC 42 family (42001, 23894,
23053, and others). It is the reference most other AI standards point to for definitions.

## What it covers
- **Core definitions** — *AI system*, *machine learning*, *model*, *training/inference*,
  *AI agent*, *autonomy*, and related terms, intended for consistent cross-standard use.
- **AI concepts** — the AI system **life cycle** (inception, design and development,
  verification and validation, deployment, operation and monitoring, re-evaluation,
  retirement), data concepts, and roles in the AI ecosystem (e.g., AI provider, AI
  producer, AI customer, AI subject).
- **Functional view** — characteristics frequently referenced by governance frameworks,
  such as transparency, robustness, and the distinction between AI development and use.

## Why it matters here
Definitional queries ("what is an AI system?", "what is a high-risk AI system?") are
grounded against shared terminology. Note that some regimes use their **own** definitions —
e.g., the **EU AI Act** and the **OECD** AI-system definition (revised 2024) — so answers
should cite the regime in question rather than assume ISO terminology applies everywhere.

## Cross-references

- **EU AI Act, Art. 3** — legally binding EU definitions.
- **ISO/IEC 42001 / 23894 / 23053** — consumers of this terminology.
