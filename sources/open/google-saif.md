# Google Secure AI Framework (SAIF)

Google's **Secure AI Framework (SAIF)** is a conceptual, practitioner-oriented framework for
building and deploying AI systems securely — translating Google's experience defending AI at
scale into reusable security guidance across the AI development lifecycle.

## Six core elements
1. **Expand strong security foundations to the AI ecosystem** — apply secure-by-default
   infrastructure protections and expertise to AI systems.
2. **Extend detection and response** to bring AI into the organization's threat universe —
   monitor generative-AI inputs/outputs to detect anomalies and threats.
3. **Automate defenses** to keep pace with existing and new threats — use AI itself to scale
   incident response.
4. **Harmonize platform-level controls** to ensure consistent security across the organization
   — standardize controls and frameworks rather than fragmenting them per model.
5. **Adapt controls** to adjust mitigations and create faster feedback loops for AI deployment —
   continuous testing (e.g. red-teaming), retraining, and adjustment.
6. **Contextualize AI system risks** in surrounding business processes — end-to-end risk
   assessments covering data lineage, validation, and operational behaviour.

## AI-specific risks addressed
SAIF organizes AI security around concrete risk categories spanning the supply chain and
operational lifecycle — including **data poisoning**, **prompt injection**, **model source
tampering / exfiltration**, **sensitive-data disclosure**, **model evasion**, and **insecure
agent/tool use**. It maps each risk to protective controls.

## Tools
- **SAIF Risk Map** — an interactive view of the AI security landscape and where risks arise.
- **Self-Assessment** — a questionnaire that surfaces the risks relevant to an organization and
  the controls to address them.

## SAIF 2.0 — agent security
The 2.0 evolution focuses on **securing autonomous AI agents** ("building powerful agents users
can trust"), addressing agent-specific attack surfaces (tool use, memory, multi-step autonomy)
and behavioural risks.
