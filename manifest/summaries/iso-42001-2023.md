## Scope and purpose
ISO/IEC 42001 is the first certifiable **AI management system (AIMS)** standard. It
specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually
improving a management system for the responsible development and use of AI within an
organization. It is analogous in form to ISO/IEC 27001 (information security) and ISO 9001
(quality): a process/governance framework, not a technical product specification. It is
intended for any organization that provides or uses AI, regardless of size or sector.

## Structure (Harmonized Structure, Clauses 4–10)
Like other ISO management-system standards, 42001 follows the common Harmonized Structure.
The auditable requirements live in clauses 4–10:

- **Clause 4 — Context of the organization.** Understand internal/external issues,
  interested parties, and define the AIMS scope.
- **Clause 5 — Leadership.** Top-management commitment, an AI policy, and assigned roles
  and responsibilities.
- **Clause 6 — Planning.** Actions to address risks and opportunities, **AI risk
  assessment**, **AI system impact assessment**, and AI objectives.
- **Clause 7 — Support.** Resources, competence, awareness, communication, and documented
  information.
- **Clause 8 — Operation.** Operational planning and control; carrying out the AI risk and
  impact assessments.
- **Clause 9 — Performance evaluation.** Monitoring, measurement, internal audit, and
  management review.
- **Clause 10 — Improvement.** Nonconformity, corrective action, and continual improvement.

## Annex A — reference controls (themes)
Annex A provides a set of reference controls and implementation guidance (with Annex B
guidance and Annex C describing potential AI-related organizational objectives and risk
sources). The controls are organized around themes such as: AI policies; internal
organization and roles; resources for AI systems (data, tooling, human, computing);
**AI impact assessment**; the **AI system life cycle** (responsible design and
development); **data for AI systems**; information for interested parties; use of AI
systems; and management of third-party and customer relationships. Organizations select
applicable controls via a Statement of Applicability, mirroring the ISO/IEC 27001 pattern.

## How to implement (high level)
1. Define the AIMS scope and stakeholders (Clause 4).
2. Secure leadership commitment and publish an AI policy; assign roles (Clause 5).
3. Run AI **risk** and **impact** assessments; set objectives and a treatment plan
   (Clause 6, Annex A impact-assessment controls).
4. Stand up supporting processes — competence, documentation, lifecycle controls
   (Clauses 7–8, Annex A lifecycle/data controls).
5. Monitor, audit, and review; drive continual improvement (Clauses 9–10).
6. Optionally pursue third-party **certification** against the standard.

## Cross-references

- **ISO/IEC 22989** — terminology used throughout.
- **ISO/IEC 38507** — board/governing-body governance view that sits above the AIMS.
- **NIST AI RMF** — voluntary US framework that maps closely onto 42001's govern/measure/

- **EU AI Act** — a 42001-conformant AIMS can support, but does not by itself satisfy,
  AI Act obligations (e.g., Art. 9 risk management for high-risk systems).
