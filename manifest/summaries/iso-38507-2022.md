# ISO/IEC 38507:2022 — Governance Implications of the Use of AI by Organizations

> **ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 40** under the ISO/IEC 38500 IT-governance family.

## Scope and purpose
ISO/IEC 38507 guides the **governing body** (board / directors / equivalent) on the
governance implications of using AI within the organization. It is aimed at oversight and
direction — *not* at the operational management system. It helps leaders understand what
questions to ask, what to be accountable for, and how AI affects existing governance
duties. It sits within the **ISO/IEC 38500** governance-of-IT framework
(Evaluate–Direct–Monitor).

## What it covers
- **Governance vs management** — the governing body **governs** (sets direction, ensures
  accountability) while management **implements**; 38507 addresses the former, ISO/IEC
  42001 the latter.
- **Evaluate–Direct–Monitor** — applying the ISO/IEC 38500 model to AI: evaluate
  AI-related opportunities and risks, direct the organization's approach (policy,
  acceptable use), and monitor outcomes and compliance.
- **Governance considerations** — accountability, organizational values and ethics,
  policy, transparency and explainability expectations, data governance, risk and
  compliance obligations, and the human/societal impacts of AI use.

## Cross-references

- **ISO/IEC 42001** — the management system the governing body directs and monitors.
